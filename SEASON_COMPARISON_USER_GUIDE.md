# Season Comparison View - User Guide

## Quick Start

### Accessing the Feature
1. Log in as a player
2. You'll see your Player Dashboard
3. Click the **"📈 Compare"** tab in the navigation bar

## Using Multi-Season Comparison

### Step 1: Select Seasons to Compare
- Click the **"Select Seasons"** dropdown
- Check 2 or more seasons you want to compare
- The comparison table will update automatically

### Step 2: Review Comparison Table
The table shows key statistics side-by-side:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Games Played | Total games bowled in that season |
| Average | Your average score per game |
| High Game | Your best single game score |
| High Series | Your best 3-game series total |
| Total Pins | Combined pins knocked down |

**Color Indicators:**
- 🟢 **Green**: Your best performance across selected seasons
- 🔴 **Red**: Your weakest performance across selected seasons

### Example Comparison
```
Season           | Games | Average | High Game | High Series | Total Pins
Fall 2024        |   24  |   185   |    245    |     690     |   4,440
Summer 2024      |   18  |   178   |    238    |     665     |   3,204
Spring 2024      |   21  |   192   |    256    |     715     |   4,032
```
In this example:
- Best average: Spring 2024 (192) - shown in green
- Highest single game: Spring 2024 (256) - shown in green
- Most games played: Fall 2024 (24)

## Using the Performance Chart

### Step 1: Select a Season
- Use the **"Select Season for Chart"** dropdown
- Choose the season you want to analyze in detail

### Step 2: Interpret the Chart

#### Visual Elements
1. **Blue Bars**: Each bar represents one game score
   - Height shows the score (taller = higher score)
   - Games ordered chronologically (left to right)

2. **Red Line**: Your running average
   - Shows how your average evolved throughout the season
   - Helps identify improvement or decline trends

3. **Hover Tooltips**: Mouse over any bar to see:
   - Game number
   - Exact score
   - Date played
   - Teams involved
   - Your team name

#### Stats Cards (Above Chart)
- **Games Played**: Total games in this season
- **Current Average**: Your season average
- **High Game**: Best single game score
- **Low Game**: Lowest single game score

### Example Analysis

**Scenario**: Viewing Fall 2024 Season Chart

```
Game 1: 165 (Red line starts at 165)
Game 2: 188 (Red line rises to 176.5)
Game 3: 201 (Red line rises to 184.7)
...
Game 24: 219 (Red line ends at 185)
```

**Insights from this chart:**
- Started slow (165) but improved over time
- Finished strong with 219
- Consistent upward trend in running average
- Overall season average: 185

## Tips for Using the Feature

### Comparing Seasons
✅ **Do:**
- Compare consecutive seasons to track improvement
- Look for patterns in high games across seasons
- Note how your average changes with practice

❌ **Don't:**
- Compare seasons from different leagues (rules may differ)
- Compare incomplete seasons to completed ones
- Ignore the context (injuries, life changes, etc.)

### Reading the Chart
✅ **Do:**
- Look for trends (upward/downward slopes)
- Identify consistency (are scores clustered or scattered?)
- Note your starting vs ending average

❌ **Don't:**
- Obsess over single bad games
- Ignore external factors (new equipment, lane conditions)
- Compare yourself negatively to others

### Setting Goals
**Use the comparison to:**
1. **Identify Strengths**: What seasons/periods were your best?
2. **Spot Weaknesses**: Where did you struggle?
3. **Track Progress**: Are you improving over time?
4. **Set Targets**: Aim to beat your previous season's average

## Common Questions

### Q: Why don't I see any seasons?
**A:** You need to have completed at least 1 game in a season for it to appear. Seasons in "setup" status won't show statistics yet.

### Q: Can I compare more than 3 seasons?
**A:** Yes! Select as many seasons as you want. The table will scroll if there are too many columns.

### Q: What if I was absent for games?
**A:** Absent games are counted as (average - 10) per league rules. They appear in your statistics but are marked clearly in the data.

### Q: How is the running average calculated?
**A:** It's calculated after each game:
- Game 1: Average = Game 1 score
- Game 2: Average = (Game 1 + Game 2) / 2
- Game 3: Average = (Game 1 + Game 2 + Game 3) / 3
- And so on...

### Q: Why does my chart look different from my teammate's?
**A:** Each player has their own unique performance pattern. The chart scales to your scores, so a 200 game might look different heights for different players.

### Q: Can I export this data?
**A:** Not yet - this is a future enhancement! For now, you can take screenshots or manually record the data you need.

## Troubleshooting

### Chart Not Showing
**Possible Causes:**
- No season selected in dropdown
- Selected season has no completed games
- Browser compatibility issue

**Solutions:**
1. Select a different season
2. Check that games were actually completed
3. Try refreshing the page

### Comparison Table Empty
**Possible Causes:**
- No seasons selected in dropdown
- No completed seasons available

**Solutions:**
1. Select seasons from the dropdown
2. Complete at least 1 game in a season
3. Ask your league admin to mark games as completed

### Performance Issues
**If the page is slow:**
- You likely have many seasons (this is good!)
- Try comparing fewer seasons at once
- Close other browser tabs

## Privacy & Data

**Your data is:**
- Stored locally in your browser (localStorage)
- Not sent to any external servers
- Visible only to you when logged in as a player
- Visible to league admins for game management

**Admins can:**
- See all player statistics
- Mark games as completed
- Manage league settings

**Admins cannot:**
- Access your personal account
- Modify your personal data without league context

## Need Help?

Contact your league administrator if you:
- Don't see expected data
- Notice incorrect statistics
- Need historical data adjusted
- Have questions about scoring rules
